How to Play Hand & Foot

Set-up

Each player has a full deck (ie: 4 players have 4 decks) of 54 cards (including 2 Jokers. See image above). All cards are shuffled together and dealt into piles of 15 cards that number double the number of players (ie: if there are 4 players, there will be 8 piles). Each player chooses 2 piles. The player chooses one pile to become the hand while the other becomes the foot. It doesn’t matter which pile becomes what. The foot is placed face-down to the side and the hand becomes your hand to start the game. The remaining cards are placed in the center of the play area as a draw pile. The top card is removed and placed face-up next to the draw pile as the beginning of the discard pile. Arbitrarily determine the player that starts the game. I recommend having the player with the next birthday go first but you can choose your own favorite method.

Turn Structure

To start the turn, draw from either the draw pile or the discard pile (If drawing from the discard pile, you take the whole discard pile). Next, play all cards that you can and want to play. Note: In order to play your first set the number of points in the cards played must exceed 30 points for every round that has been started in the game (ex: 30 points in the first round, 60 points in the second round, etc.). Please refer to ‘Sets’ for more information. Discard a card to end the turn. Play progresses in the clockwise direction.

Ending the Round

To end the round, a player must achieve all of the following:

  • 2 closed clean sets
  • 2 closed dirty sets
  • No cards remaining in the Foot
  • No cards remaining in the Hand
When these conditions are met and the player ends their turn, the round ends immediately. All scores are tallied and recorded before the next round begins.

Ending the Game

The game concludes once the desired number of rounds is finished (the standard is 3 rounds). After the final round is tallied, each player's total points from all rounds are added together. The player with the highest total score wins the game!
