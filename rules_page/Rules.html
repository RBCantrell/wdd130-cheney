Overview

The game of Hand & Foot is a rummy style card game with unique features that presents a satisfying challenge. This game is not the easiest to learn, nor is it for the faint of heart. The game of Hand & Foot requires multiple full decks of cards and either a great deal of shuffling skills or a card shuffling machine that can handle over 100 cards at a time. It is recommended that the decks used are the same size while having card art that is different enough to differentiate for easier separation at the end of the game. Bicycle decks are a good standard. It is also recommended that you include a calculator for the scoring following each round.

Sets

A set is a grouping of cards of a kind (ex: a set of four 5’s, a set of six 9’s). A set is dirty if it includes at least one wild (Ace or Joker) otherwise it is clean. A clean set can be made dirty by adding a wild. A dirty set cannot be made clean. A dirty set can have at most half of its cards be wilds. The minimum number of cards a set can have is 3. To close a set, there must be 5 cards in the set. You cannot add to any set that isn’t your own.

The Foot

The foot is a second hand that remains out of play until your hand is empty. If you have not ended your turn to empty your hand (ie: If you did not discard your last card) you may pick up your foot use the cards as your new hand immediately. If you did discard to empty your hand, you must wait until your next turn to use the cards in your foot.
